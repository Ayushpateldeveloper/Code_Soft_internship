A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931 - 2015

Missile Man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam 15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.[1] He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.[2][3][4] He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

Early Life and Education

Abdul Kalam was the youngest of five siblings and was raised in a devout Muslim family. Despite facing financial challenges, his parents instilled in him strong values of honesty, discipline, and education. His father was a boat owner and imam at the local mosque, while his mother was a housewife. Kalam had a keen interest in science from a young age. After completing his early education at a local school, he attended the Ramanathapuram Schwartz Matriculation School.His teachers recognized his inquisitive mind and encouraged him to pursue his passion for learning.In 1954, Kalam graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli. He then enrolled in the Madras Institute of Technology, where he specialized in aeronautical engineering.

Scientific Career

After completing his studies, Abdul Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He made significant contributions to India's space and missile development programs, particularly in the design and development of launch vehicles. His pioneering work on the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV) laid the foundation for India's successful space endeavors.One of the defining moments of his scientific career came in 1998 when India conducted its nuclear tests. As the chief scientific advisor to the Prime Minister, Kalam played a crucial role in ensuring the success of these tests, solidifying India's position as a nuclear power.

The People's President

In 2002, APJ Abdul Kalam became the President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. His presidency was marked by his genuine connection with the people and his relentless efforts to inspire the youth. He often engaged with students and young minds, delivering motivational speeches that left a lasting impact on everyone who heard him.Kalam was affectionately called the "People's President" due to his approachable and humble nature. He used his position to advocate for education, especially in science and technology, and promoted research and innovation to drive India's progress.

Vision for India

Throughout his life, Kalam emphasized the importance of self-reliance, technological advancement, and inclusive growth. He believed that science and technology could be leveraged to solve many of India's problems, from poverty and healthcare to sustainable development and national security. Kalam had a vision for India to become a developed nation by the year 2020. He outlined this vision in his book "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," where he laid out a roadmap for India's transformation into a knowledge-driven economy.

Legacy and Inspirational Quotes

APJ Abdul Kalam's legacy extends far beyond his scientific achievements and political career. His positive attitude, humble demeanor, and words of wisdom continue to resonate with people of all ages. Some of his most famous quotes include:


  • Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts, and thoughts result in action.
  • If you want to shine like a sun, first burn like a sun.
  • Don't take rest after your first victory because if you fail in the second, more lips are waiting to say that your first victory was just luck.

Conclusion

APJ Abdul Kalam's life and accomplishments serve as an inspiration to countless individuals across the globe. He proved that with determination, hard work, and the pursuit of knowledge, anyone can rise above their circumstances and make a significant impact on society. His vision for a prosperous and self-reliant India continues to guide the nation's aspirations, and his messages of hope and perseverance echo in the hearts of those who strive to make the world a better place.As we pay tribute to this extraordinary individual, let us remember his words and emulate his dedication to excellence, innovation, and service to humanity. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam will forever remain an icon of inspiration, a visionary leader, and a true gem in the history of India and the world.
